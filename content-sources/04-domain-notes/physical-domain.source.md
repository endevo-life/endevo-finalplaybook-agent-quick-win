# Physical domain (health & caregiving) — deterministic questions (source)

Same tiered pattern as Financial: each answer → intro + review-checklist or steps.

---

## P1 — Body, medical coverage, donation, funeral home

### P1.1 If you died unexpectedly tomorrow, have you documented what you want done with your body (burial, cremation, aquamation, green burial, body donation)?

- **Yes, written, signed, and stored with my legal documents** → *review*
  - Checklist: instructions still exactly what you want? new options (aquamation/green burial) now preferred? trusted person knows where document is stored? will/Letter of Instruction updated to match?
- **I have decided but it is not in writing** → *steps*
  1. Write down your preferred option (burial, cremation, aquamation, green burial, body donation). Even a simple statement is legally helpful.
  2. Add any details that matter (location, ceremony preferences, donation instructions).
  3. Store the written instructions with your legal documents.
  4. Tell your trusted person where the document is.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Decide what you want done with your body.
  2. Write down your choice clearly and simply.
  3. Store the document with your legal papers.
  4. Tell your trusted person where it is and what it says.
  5. Update your will or Letter of Instruction so everything matches.

### P1.2 If you needed urgent or long-term medical care tomorrow, could your trusted person locate your health insurance card, Medicare/Medicaid info, supplemental coverage, and any pre-existing condition records within an hour?

- **Yes, all of it is in one accessible place they know about** → *review*
  - Checklist: cards/documents still in same place? changed plans/providers/coverage recently? pre-existing condition records up to date? trusted person knows how to access everything?
- **They could find some of it but not all** → *steps*
  1. Gather all insurance documents (health insurance card, Medicare/Medicaid, supplemental coverage).
  2. Collect pre-existing condition records or summaries.
  3. Store everything in one secure, accessible location.
  4. Tell your trusted person exactly where documents are and how to access them.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Gather health insurance card, Medicare/Medicaid, supplemental coverage.
  2. Collect pre-existing condition records/summaries.
  3. Store everything together, secure but easy to reach.
  4. Tell your trusted person where documents are and how to access them.
  5. Review and update the folder annually.

### P1.3 If you died unexpectedly tomorrow, would your family know within hours whether you are an organ donor — and is it documented on your driver's license or state registry?

- **Yes, documented on license or registry and family knows my wishes** → *review*
  - Checklist: donor status still on license? registered with state registry? family knows your wishes clearly? related medical/legal documents updated?
- **I have decided but it is not on my license or my family does not know** → *steps*
  1. Register your decision with your state's registry or update your driver's license.
  2. Tell your family exactly what you want.
  3. Add your donor preference to medical documents or Letter of Instruction.
  4. Store written confirmation with legal papers.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Decide whether you want to be an organ donor.
  2. Register your choice or update your driver's license.
  3. Tell your family what you've decided.
  4. Store written confirmation with legal documents.
  5. Review your decision periodically.

### P1.4 If you died unexpectedly tomorrow, would your family know exactly which funeral home to call within the first 24 hours?

- **Yes, funeral home selected and family knows the name** → *review*
  - Checklist: still your preferred choice? current phone/address documented? family remembers name and knows where info is stored? will/Letter of Instruction updated?
- **I have researched options but have not selected one or told family** → *steps*
  1. Pick the funeral home you prefer.
  2. Write down name, phone number, address.
  3. Store this with legal documents or Letter of Instruction.
  4. Tell your family your choice.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Research funeral homes and choose one matching preferences/budget.
  2. Write down name, phone, address.
  3. Store with legal documents.
  4. Tell your family your choice.
  5. Update will/Letter of Instruction to match.

## P2 — Advance directives, interventions, palliative/hospice, good death

### P2.1 If you were unconscious in an ICU after an accident tomorrow, do you have a Medical Advance Directive (Living Will) telling doctors what interventions you want or do not want?

- **Yes, signed and on file with my doctor and Healthcare POA** → *review*
  - Checklist: directive still what you want? doctor has most recent version? Healthcare POA knows where original is stored? related docs updated (HIPAA release, POLST, DNR)?
- **I have started one but it is not signed or distributed** → *steps*
  1. Finish writing your directive (ventilation, resuscitation, feeding tubes, comfort-only care).
  2. Sign per your state's requirements (witnesses or notarization).
  3. Give copies to doctor, hospital system, Healthcare POA.
  4. Store the original securely and tell your Healthcare POA where it is.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Decide what interventions you'd want or decline (ventilation, CPR, feeding tubes, comfort-only).
  2. Write your choices in a state directive form.
  3. Sign with required witnesses or a notary.
  4. Give copies to doctor, hospital, Healthcare POA.
  5. Store the original securely and accessibly.

### P2.2 If you got a terminal diagnosis tomorrow, have you documented exactly which medical interventions you DO and DO NOT want during your active dying phase (CPR, intubation, feeding tube, comfort-care only)?

- **Yes, documented in writing and shared with my Healthcare POA and doctor** → *review*
  - Checklist: instructions still what you want? doctor has most recent version? POA knows where original is? related docs updated (Advance Directive, POLST, DNR)?
- **I have general preferences but nothing specific or documented** → *steps*
  1. Decide clearly which interventions you want or decline.
  2. Write choices in a formal Advance Directive or Living Will.
  3. Sign per state requirements.
  4. Give copies to doctor, hospital, Healthcare POA.
  5. Store original securely and tell your POA where it is.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Learn what each intervention means (CPR, intubation, feeding tube, comfort-care only).
  2. Write choices in a state-approved directive/Living Will.
  3. Sign with witnesses or notary.
  4. Give copies to doctor and Healthcare POA.
  5. Store original securely and review periodically.

### P2.3 If you got a terminal diagnosis tomorrow, do you understand the difference between palliative care and hospice — and which one you would want and when?

- **Yes, I understand both and have documented my preference** → *review*
  - Checklist: written preference still matches? Advance Directive/Living Will updated? POA knows palliative vs hospice timing? doctor has most recent version?
- **I know the terms but I am fuzzy on the difference or what I would want** → *steps*
  1. Learn the distinction — palliative care can begin at any stage of serious illness (comfort + symptom relief while still receiving curative treatment); hospice is for the final months when curative treatment is no longer pursued (comfort and dignity).
  2. Decide when you'd want each type of care.
  3. Document preferences in Advance Directive or Living Will.
  4. Share decisions with Healthcare POA and doctor.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Understand palliative care (comfort/symptom relief at any stage, even alongside treatment).
  2. Understand hospice (comfort-focused care for final months when treatment isn't pursued).
  3. Decide which approach and when.
  4. Write preferences in an Advance Directive/Living Will.
  5. Share with Healthcare POA and doctor.

### P2.4 If you got a terminal diagnosis tomorrow, do the people closest to you know what a good death looks like to you (where you want to be, who you want present, what matters most in your final days)?

- **Yes, I have had this conversation with my loved ones** → *review*
  - Checklist: loved ones remember the details? preferences about location changed? new people to include? wishes written in Letter of Instruction/Advance Directive?
- **I have thought about it but have not talked to them** → *steps*
  1. Reflect on what matters most (location, atmosphere, people present, spiritual/emotional needs).
  2. Choose a calm moment to talk with loved ones.
  3. Write down wishes in a Letter of Instruction or Advance Directive.
  4. Store securely and tell loved ones where it is.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Reflect on what matters most (comfort, location, people, rituals, music, quiet, spiritual support).
  2. Write down preferences.
  3. Share with the people closest to you.
  4. Add to a Letter of Instruction/Advance Directive.
  5. Review periodically as values evolve.

## P3 — Long-term care & dementia preferences

### P3.1 If you needed long-term care tomorrow, have you documented your preferences (in-home care, assisted living, memory care, specific facility, who you would want as caregiver)?

- **Yes, written down and shared with my Healthcare POA** → *review*
  - Checklist: preferred settings same? specific facilities/caregivers identified? POA has most recent version? medical/financial circumstances changed?
- **I have general preferences but nothing documented** → *steps*
  1. Clarify preferred care setting (in-home, assisted living, memory care, specific facility).
  2. Identify who you'd want involved (family, professional caregivers, agency).
  3. Write preferences in a Letter of Instruction or Advance Directive.
  4. Share with Healthcare POA.
  5. Store the original securely.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Learn differences between in-home care, assisted living, memory care.
  2. Reflect on what matters most (independence, safety, familiarity, social support, memory care).
  3. Write preferences in a Letter of Instruction/Advance Directive.
  4. Share with Healthcare POA.
  5. Review periodically.

### P3.2 If you began experiencing cognitive decline or dementia tomorrow, have you discussed with your Healthcare POA what level of intervention you would want as your condition progresses?

- **Yes, we have talked through specific stages and decisions** → *review*
  - Checklist: preferences match at early/moderate/advanced stages? Advance Directive/care plan updated? POA remembers details + has written copies? new concerns to add?
- **We have talked generally but not about specific stages or decisions** → *steps*
  1. Learn the typical stages of cognitive decline (mild, moderate, advanced).
  2. Decide the level of intervention at each stage (safety measures, medication, in-home care, assisted living, memory care, limits on aggressive interventions).
  3. Write preferences in a Letter of Instruction/Advance Directive.
  4. Share with Healthcare POA and talk through each stage.
  5. Store securely and tell POA where it is.
- **No** → *steps*
  1. Learn how dementia typically progresses.
  2. Reflect on what matters most (independence, safety, comfort, familiar surroundings, memory care).
  3. Write preferences in a Letter of Instruction/Advance Directive.
  4. Discuss with Healthcare POA.
  5. Review periodically.
