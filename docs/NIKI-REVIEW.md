## 1. The 11 scenario questions (the personalization "why now" clusters)

These appear only when a member picks the matching "why now?" signal. Each was
drafted to match your existing question style. Review the wording + whether the
routing makes sense.

### GUARD_named  (legal)
**Q:** If both parents were gone tomorrow, is there a legally named guardian who would raise your children?

- **Yes, a guardian is named in a signed document** -> Review your guardian choice so it still reflects who you'd want and their current willingness.
- **We've talked about it, but nothing is written down** -> Put your guardian choice in writing so a court would honor it, instead of deciding for your children.
- **No, no guardian has been named** -> Name a guardian for your children so the choice is yours, not a court's.

### GUARD_provision  (financial)
**Q:** If you were gone, is there money set aside so whoever raises your children isn't doing it out of pocket?

- **Yes, life insurance and/or a trust is in place** -> Review that the provision for your children is current and points to the right people.
- **No, there's no plan for their financial care** -> Set up a way for your children to be provided for if you're gone.

### BIZ_succession  (financial)
**Q:** If you couldn't run your business tomorrow, is there a written plan for who takes over and how?

- **Yes, there's a documented succession plan** -> Review your succession plan so it matches how the business runs today.
- **There's an idea, but nothing formal** -> Turn your succession idea into a written plan so the business survives a sudden gap.
- **No, the business depends entirely on me** -> Create a basic continuity plan so your business isn't lost if you can't run it.

### PHYS_proxy  (physical)
**Q:** If you couldn't speak for yourself in a medical crisis, does one specific person have the legal authority to decide for you?

- **Yes, a healthcare proxy/agent is named in writing** -> Review your healthcare proxy so the right person can act without friction.
- **No, no one is formally authorized** -> Name a healthcare agent so someone you trust can decide for you in a crisis.

### LOSS_gaps  (legal)
**Q:** After losing someone close, was there something they didn't leave behind that made it harder — access, wishes, or documents?

- **Yes — and I don't want that for my people** -> Capture the specific gaps you saw, so your own plan closes them first.
- **It was hard, but I'm not sure what was missing** -> Review the moments that were hardest, to surface what your own plan should include.

### ESTATE_executor  (legal)
**Q:** Having settled an estate, do you have your own executor named — and have you spared them what you went through?

- **Yes — my executor is named and equipped** -> Review that your executor has what you wished you'd had.
- **No — I haven't set up my own** -> Name your executor and give them the roadmap you didn't have.

### CHANGE_beneficiaries  (financial)
**Q:** After your recent life change, have you re-checked who inherits — beneficiaries, titles, and who's named on your documents?

- **Yes — I've updated everything to match** -> Review that every document reflects your life as it is now.
- **No — some things may still name the old situation** -> Update your beneficiaries and named roles so nothing routes to the wrong person.

### AGE_sweep  (legal)
**Q:** You've hit a milestone age. Do you have the three basics in place — a will, a healthcare proxy, and a financial POA?

- **Yes — all three are current** -> Review the three basics so they still reflect your wishes.
- **I have one or two, not all** -> Complete the missing basics so all three are in place.
- **None yet** -> Put the three foundational documents in place.

### DIGITAL_inventory  (digital)
**Q:** Since most of your life is online, is there a single place your trusted person could find every important account and how to reach it?

- **Yes — I keep a maintained list / password manager** -> Review your digital inventory so it's complete and reachable.
- **No — it's scattered across my devices and memory** -> Build one digital inventory so nothing important is lost or locked away.

### PUBLIC_worry  (legal)
**Q:** A story that shook you usually points at a specific fear. Is the thing you worried could happen to your family actually covered in your plan?

- **No — that exact gap is open for me** -> Close the specific gap the story exposed, before anything else.
- **I think so, but I want to be sure** -> Pressure-test the area the story worried you about.

### UNFINISHED_named  (legal)
**Q:** You're here because something's been unfinished too long. What's the one thing that's been nagging you?

- **A will / who gets what** -> Finish the will that's been waiting.
- **Medical wishes / who decides for me** -> Put your healthcare wishes and proxy in place.
- **Access to my accounts / passwords** -> Close the access gap that's been open.
- **Something else** -> Name it and take the first concrete step.

## 2. The glossary (27 terms Jesse uses to answer questions)

Jesse (the chat guide) now explains these when asked. The ones you flagged
(will vs trust) are here. Review each definition for accuracy + tone.

| Term | Domain | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Will | legal | A legal document that names your executor and states who receives your assets after you die. |
| Power of attorney (financial) | legal | A legal document that lets a trusted person make financial decisions on your behalf while you're alive but unable to make them yourself. |
| Power of attorney (healthcare) / medical advance directive | health | A legal document naming who can make medical decisions for you if you can't, and stating your care wishes in detail. |
| DNR (Do Not Resuscitate) | health | A medical order stating you do not want CPR performed if your heart stops or you stop breathing. |
| Executor | legal | The person legally responsible for carrying out your will after you die — settling accounts, distributing assets, closing the estate. |
| Transfer-on-death (TOD) | financial | A designation on a brokerage or investment account that names who receives it directly when you die, skipping probate. |
| Payable-on-death (POD) | financial | Like transfer-on-death, but for bank accounts — names who receives the account balance directly when you die. |
| Beneficiary designation | financial | The person or people named on an account (401k, life insurance, IRA) to receive it after you die. These override what your will says, and don't update automatically. |
| Probate | legal | The court process of settling an estate when accounts or property don't have a named beneficiary or designation — slower and more public than a direct transfer. |
| Digital legacy / digital assets | digital | Your online accounts, files, and business logins — who has access, and what happens to them, if you're gone. |
| Guardian | legal | The person legally named to raise your minor children if you (and your partner) are no longer able to. |
| Trust | legal | A legal arrangement where a trustee holds and manages assets for your beneficiaries. Unlike a will, a living trust can let assets pass to your people WITHOUT going through probate court, and can take effect while you're alive if you become unable to manage things yourself. |
| Will vs. trust | legal | A will takes effect only after you die and usually goes through probate (a public court process) before assets are distributed. A living trust can avoid probate, works if you're incapacitated (not just at death), and stays private. Many people have BOTH: a will as a backstop, and a trust for the main assets. Which fits you depends on your situation. An estate attorney can advise. |
| Living will | legal | A document that states the medical care you do or don't want if you can't speak for yourself (for example, life support). Different from a regular will, which is about your assets after death. |
| Estate | legal | Everything you own at death: property, accounts, belongings, and digital assets. Your estate is what gets distributed by your will, trust, or, if you have neither, by state law. |
| Intestate | legal | Dying without a valid will. When that happens, your state's laws decide who inherits your assets, which may not match what you would have wanted. |
| Beneficiary | financial | The person or organization you name to receive an asset (like a retirement account or life insurance) when you die. Beneficiary designations usually override what your will says, so keeping them current matters. |
| Life insurance | financial | A policy that pays a chosen beneficiary a sum of money when you die. It can help your family cover costs and replace lost income. Confirm the named beneficiary is still who you intend. |
| Financial inventory | financial | A single organized list of your accounts, policies, debts, and where to find them, so the person handling your affairs isn't searching blind. |
| Healthcare proxy / agent | physical | The person you legally name to make medical decisions for you if you can't make them yourself. Also called a healthcare power of attorney. |
| Advance directive | physical | A general term for the documents that spell out your medical wishes and who decides for you: typically a living will plus a healthcare proxy. |
| Five Wishes | physical | A widely used, plain-language advance directive that covers medical, personal, emotional, and spiritual wishes, not just legal ones. |
| Legacy contact | digital | A person you designate with Apple, Google, or Facebook who can access or manage your account after you die. Setting one up is one of the simplest, highest-impact digital steps. |
| Password manager | digital | A secure app that stores your logins in one place. It lets a trusted person reach your accounts (with the right access) instead of being locked out. |
| Digital assets | digital | Anything you own or control online: accounts, photos, email, social media, crypto, domains, and files. Without a plan, much of it can be lost or inaccessible to your family. |
| Inactive Account Manager | digital | Google's tool to decide what happens to your account and data if you stop using it, including who can be notified or given access. |
| Ethical will / legacy letter | beliefs | A personal (non-legal) letter sharing your values, stories, and wishes for your loved ones. Not legally binding, but often the most treasured thing you leave behind. |
