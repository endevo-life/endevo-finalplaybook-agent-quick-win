Product Video · Blueprint & Production Guide

My Final Playbook — the 2-minute film

One video, two acts: an honest, warm family story that opens the heart, then a calm walkthrough of the real product that answers "so what do I actually do?" Every word of voiceover is paired with the exact visual it should sit over — so what you hear always matches what you see.

Live Fully ~ Die Ready.
Runtime: ~2:00 Act 1: Emotional open (0:00–0:40) Act 2: Product demo (0:40–2:00) Visuals: AI-generated Aspect: 16:9 master (+ 9:16 cutdowns)
Brand rules baked into this script No em-dashes in anything on screen or spoken. Plain, kitchen-table language (no "probate," "intestate," "fiduciary" unexplained). Candid about death without dread: warm, human, never morbid, clinical, or salesy. We educate, we do not advise. Product name is written in full: My Final Playbook. Tagline uses a tilde: Live Fully ~ Die Ready.

01The shape of the film

Two acts, one arc: from the quiet fear of leaving your family locked out, to the relief of a plan they can actually find and follow.

Act 1 · 0:00–0:40
The worry, named gently

A real family moment. Warm home light. The unspoken question: if you were gone tomorrow, would they be able to find anything? We name the fear, then offer a hand, not a lecture.

Act 2 · 0:40–2:00
The plan, made simple

The real product on screen. Answer a few plain questions, see your prioritized plan across Legal, Financial, Physical, and Digital, check items off, and hand your family a finished, sealed playbook.

04How to actually make it — tools & connectors

A production pipeline that fits what you have connected. Each stage names the tool, what it makes, and how it hands off to the next.

Stage 1
Stills

Generate the Act 1 family scenes + still-life shots from the AI image prompts in each storyboard scene.

  • Use an image generator (Midjourney, DALL·E, or your image-gen connector). Keep one consistent family + one consistent home across all Act 1 prompts by reusing a seed or character reference.
  • Render 16:9. Generate 2–3 variants per scene and pick the warmest, most candid.

Consistency tip Lead every prompt with the same style suffix ("documentary style, teal and cream palette, soft natural light, film grain, no text") so all shots feel like one film.

Stage 2
Motion

Turn stills into gentle moving shots (the slow push-ins and cross-dissolves).

  • Use an image-to-video tool (Runway, Pika, Kling, or Luma) to add subtle camera motion to each still. Keep moves slow: this is a calm film.
  • Or, simplest: apply a slow Ken Burns zoom to each still in your editor. Often enough for this tone.
Stage 3
Screens

Record the Act 2 product demo from the live app.

  • Screen-record the real flow: assessment → generated plan → check-off + seal → sealed PDF. Use the aging-parent example so all four domains are full.
  • 1920x1080, clean browser. Then frame each capture on an AI-generated warm desk/cream background so the screens live inside the film's world.
Stage 4
Voice

Record or generate the voiceover from the transcript above.

  • Warm, calm, unhurried female or male voice. A real human read is ideal for this topic; if using AI voice (ElevenLabs), choose a gentle, mature timbre and slow the pace slightly.
  • The .vtt timings above are your target read pace (about 2:00 total).
Stage 5
Assemble

Edit it all together to the storyboard timings.

  • Lay the VO on the timeline first, then drop each scene's visual to match its timecode, so words and visuals lock (that is the whole point of the two-column storyboard).
  • Add the soft on-screen text beats. Add music (calm piano/strings, low under VO). You already have a music and a no-music cut of the current video for reference.
  • Attach captions.en.vtt. Export a 16:9 master.

Editors CapCut, DaVinci Resolve (free), Premiere, or Descript (which also does the VO + captions in one place).

Stage 6
Cutdowns

Make the social/ad versions from the master.

  • 9:16 vertical, 15–30s: open on the strongest line ("passwords no one else has"), one product beat (the seal), then the CTA. Hook in the first 3 seconds.
  • Burn in captions for silent autoplay. Keep the disclaimer line on the end card.
Sensitive-topic + AI-imagery cautions This is end-of-life content, so keep AI faces warm and dignified, never distressed or in a hospital. Review every generated frame for the tell-tale AI artifacts (hands, eyes, text on signs). Because these are AI-generated people, do not imply they are real customers or use them in testimonials. Keep the disclaimer "Not legal, financial, or medical advice" on the end card. Nothing spoken or shown should claim a specific dollar saved or a legal outcome.
